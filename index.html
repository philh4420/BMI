What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight that applies to adult men and women.

It is used to screen for weight categories that may lead to health problems.

BMI is calculated by dividing weight in kilograms by the square of height in meters.

The result is interpreted as follows:

  • Below 16: Severely underweight
  • 16 - 16.9: Moderately underweight
  • 17 - 18.4: Mildly underweight
  • 18.5 - 24.9: Normal weight
  • 25 - 29.9: Overweight
  • 30 - 34.9: Obesity Class I (Moderate)
  • 35 - 39.9: Obesity Class II (Severe)
  • 40 and above: Obesity Class III (Very severe or morbidly obese)

Interpreting Results

Alongside your BMI value, we provide a category to help you understand where you stand in terms of weight classification.

It's important to note that BMI is a screening tool and not a diagnostic test. It may not be accurate for individuals with high muscle mass or certain health conditions.

Always consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice regarding your weight and health.
